Why Most Neck Creams Are a Waste of Money

I called Dr. Elizabeth Chen, a board-certified dermatologist in Manhattan who specializes in aging skin, to understand why Linda's expensive creams hadn't worked.

Her answer was blunt:

"Most 'neck creams' are just repackaged face creams with fancy marketing," Dr. Chen told me. "They're not formulated for neck skin at all."

She explained that neck skin has three unique challenges:

1. It's dramatically thinner. Neck skin has 40% fewer layers than face skin. Regular creams can't penetrate deeply enough to make a difference.

2. It's chronically dehydrated. The neck has far fewer sebaceous glands. It literally can't moisturize itself the way your face can.

3. It's constantly moving. Every time you turn your head, nod, or look down, you're stretching and compressing that skin. It needs ingredients that support elasticity‚Äînot just hydration.

"If you're using a face cream on your neck," Dr. Chen said, "you're essentially putting a Band-Aid on a broken bone. The delivery system is wrong. The concentration is wrong. It's not going to work."

This is why Linda had spent over $500 on creams and seen nothing.
